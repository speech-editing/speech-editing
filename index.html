A Unified Neural Codec Language Model for Selective Editable Text to Speech Generation

Abstract

Neural codec language models achieve impressive zero-shot Text-to-Speech (TTS) by fully imitating the acoustic characteristics—timbre, prosody, and emotion—contained in a short speech prompt. However, this holistic imitation limits their ability to isolate and control specific attributes. In this letter, we present a unified codec language model that extends zero-shot TTS with a hierarchical and selective control mechanism. The model by default reproduces the complete acoustic profile inferred from the prompt; when explicit control inputs are provided, it overrides only the corresponding attributes while preserving all remaining prompt-derived properties. Timbre is controlled through a high-priority speaker embedding, while stylistic dimensions such as emotion, speaking rate, pitch, and energy are directed through textual control tags. This design enables a single end-to-end system to perform high-fidelity zero-shot TTS, prosody-preserving voice conversion, and finegrained style manipulation. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach maintains naturalness and speaker consistency while offering flexible and localized control over desired attributes.
